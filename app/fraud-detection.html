Phishing Attacks

Phishing scams are one of the most common types of fraud faced by the company's customers payment system. Phishing attacks occur when scammers create fake web pages or emails that look like pages or messages from legitimate companies, including payment processor companies.

Fraudsters may ask customers to enter their personal details such as login, password or credit card number and use these details to gain access to customer financial accounts,

To prevent phishing scams, customers should be careful when opening emails and never enter their personal details on pages that look suspicious.

In addition, the payment system company may provide additional security tools, such as two-factor authentication, which allows customers to verify their identity based on several different factors, such as a password and an SMS code.

Safety tips

1
Using strong passwords. Use a combination of letters, numbers and special characters, do not use simple words or combinations that can be easily guessed, and it is also recommended not to use the same password for different services.
2
Protecting your personal information. You should not allow an attacker or anyone else to gain access to your personal information. Do not reply to suspicious emails, do not share your passwords or credit card number through untrustworthy communication channels.
3
Using security network security, use only reliable and secure networks to access the payment service. Also, do not use unreliable wi-fi networks to perform financial transactions. Because they can be compromised by intruders.
4
Checking suspicious activity on your account It is recommended that you regularly check your account and your credit history in order to detect suspicious activity in a timely manner.
5
Two-factor authentication Use two-factor authentication to access your payment service for an extra layer of security.
